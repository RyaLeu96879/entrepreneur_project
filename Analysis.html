Analysis

After we finished modeling the phone case in Autodesk inventor, we decided to create a life size model of our product using cardboard and tape. We mainly focused on the overall details of the the product, which are the phone case and the part that holds the battery and the peltier tiles. After we cut out and taped the pieces together, we looked at how big the whole product would be. It appeared to be fairly slim, having the whole thickness of both parts being around one inch. Also, we added some details to the product, such as outlining the peltier tiles shape so we could see how much of the tiles is covered by the hands. When we did this analysis, we observed that most of the area for the peltier tiles are covered by the fingers, mainly the first and second section of the fingers. However, the peltier tiles did not have that much contact with the ends of the fingers. This leads us to believe that we should create more models for left and right handed people so that more energy can be created depending on what hand is dominant. In addition, we also discovered that the peltier tiles can be laid on the palm of the users hand. The importance of this is that the palm of the hand creates more heat, so more energy can be made this way. The overall design of the case is fine, being able to fit in someone's hand and with the added feature of being detachable, this product can create energy and be a regular phone case. Future improvements to this product would be to make the product more compact, finding less expensive peltier tiles, having a wider variety of case, finding a bigger battery that has similar dimensions, and minor design fixes.
